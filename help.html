Home Loan Eligibility Checker

Complete help manual — formulas, worked calculations, and policy reference

This tool guides you through 11 steps to assess home loan eligibility against a published policy framework. It produces a 0–100 eligibility score across 7 weighted categories, calculates live EMI and FOIR values, and maps the result to one of four outcomes: Eligible Eligible with Conditions Needs Review Not Eligible. Every threshold is defined in a single policy file — no hidden rules.

Why tenure matters beyond Step 1

Tenure feeds into the EMI formula (Step 5 display), the Age at Maturity check, and the Tenure Fit category score. A short 20-month tenure for a ₹75L loan causes a very high monthly EMI (₹4,04,371), which is the root cause of the 345.3% FOIR in this scenario.

EMI Formula — Equated Monthly Instalment

EMI Formula (Reducing Balance Method)
P × r × (1 + r)n
EMI = ──────────────────────
(1 + r)n − 1

= Monthly Equated Instalment in ₹
P

r

n
= Loan tenure in months
(1+r)^n
= Compound growth factor over n months

Rate Type (from Step 7)Rate Used for EMI EstimateActual Floating Range

Worked Example — EMI Calculation (Harshavardhana T S)
Principal (P): ₹75,00,000
Annual Rate: 8.75%
Tenure (n): 20 months
Step 1 — Monthly rate (r):r = 8.75 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.00729167
Step 2 — Compound factor:(1 + r)^n = (1.00729167)^20
=1.15637 (calculated via logarithms)
Step 3 — Numerator:P × r × (1+r)^n = 75,00,000 × 0.00729167 × 1.15637
=75,00,000 × 0.008435 = 63,262
Step 4 — Denominator:(1+r)^n − 1 = 1.15637 − 1.00000 = 0.15637
Step 5 — EMI:63,262 ÷ 0.15637 = 4,04,371
Estimated New EMI = ₹4,04,371 per month

Why is the EMI so high with a 20-month tenure?

The EMI has two components: principal repayment + interest. Over only 20 months, principal repayment alone is ₹75,00,000 ÷ 20 = ₹3,75,000/month. Interest adds ~₹29,000/month on top, giving ₹4,04,371. Compare to 30-year tenure where EMI ≈ ₹59,000 — because principal is spread over 360 months.

Floating (8.75%):EMI = ₹4,04,371 → FOIR = 345.3%
Fixed (14.00%):r = 14 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.011667
(1+r)^20 = (1.011667)^20 = 1.2594
EMI = 75,00,000 × 0.011667 × 1.2594 ÷ (1.2594 − 1)
= 75,00,000 × 0.014693 ÷ 0.2594 = ₹4,25,127
Fixed FOIR:(₹10,000 + ₹4,25,127) ÷ ₹1,20,000 = 362.6%
Fixed rate raises EMI by ₹20,756/mo (to ₹4,25,127) — FOIR worsens to 362.6% vs 345.3% floating
Rate TypeEstimation RateActual Rate RangeNotes
Floating (chosen in example) 8.75% p.a. 8.00%–9.15% p.a. Risk-based; resets with RBI repo rate. Credit score >= 750 gets lower end.
Fixed 14.00% p.a. ~14.00% p.a. Certainty of payment; higher cost vs floating.

Outcome Decision (in order of priority)

Does not meet policy criteria. Address hard fail conditions or raise the composite score significantly.
